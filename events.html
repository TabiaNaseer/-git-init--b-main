Policy Talks on S&T-03: Traditional Medicine for COVID 19: Policy and Governance of Alternative Therapeutic care

Date: 19th September 2020;Time: 4:00-5:30 pm

This session organized by Jinnah University for women in Innovation Summit 2020 is one of the sessions of its own kind as it focused a different yet unique aspect of COVID 19 about traditional medicines. Our Keynote speakers MR. Khushnood Hamdani (Diagnostic Medical Physicist (Mayoclinic USA), Dr. Halima Nazar (Professor professor in Bachelors of Eastern Medicine and Surgery department of Jinnah University for Women), and Dr. Shahlla Imam (Associate professor, Department of Pharmacognosy Institute of Pharmaceutical Sciences, Jinnah Sindh Medical University Karachi-Pakistan) shared their expertise and suggestions on the topics of “Innovation in Medical Physics, Engineering and COVID Impact”, “Boosting Immunity by natural resources in COVID-19 Era”, and “Traditional Medicines: A possible Alternative Therapeutic Care against COVID-19” respectively. The knowledge they shared can be implemented to improve the overall Health care setup including diagnosis and treatment strategies related to this pandemic. This session was hosted by Miss Qudsia Basri and Miss Syeda Zainab as a representative of Jinnah University for Women
